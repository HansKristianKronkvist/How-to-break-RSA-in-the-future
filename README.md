# How to break RSA in the future
This repository contains a copy of a case which i had as an exam last year in Quantum computing and programming. 

The case was regarding how you could break RSA encrpytion with a quantum computer and Shor's algortihm. 

I went on to show how Shor's algortihm is built as a quantum circuit, and then how the aglortihm works. 

After a short demo of the algortihm i prove that it can be broken in theory, but not on classical computers. 

My final thoughts could be summarized to thinking of encryption as a lock on your house: 
If a thief finds it very difficult to break in (such as breaking RSA on a classical computer) they wont go trough with the hassle. But if they had a key which could mold to your front door lock (Shor's algorithm on a quantum computing) they would very likely do the little work they it would take. 

Quantum computers are not such a big threat as of right now to our encryption, but in the future it can break todays encryptions in a couple of hours. Therefore it is important to find new types of encryptions safe from brute force attacks from quantum computers. Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) is such an encryption.
